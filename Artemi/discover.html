Exoplanets

An extrasolar planet, often known as an exoplanet, is a planet that is not in the Solar System. In 1992, the first exoplanet named Poltergeist and Phobetor was proven to have been discovered around a pulsar, and in 2007 j1407b or some people called it as "Super Saturn" was discovered.

White dwarfs

A white dwarf is a stellar core remnant composed mostly of electron-degenerate matter. A white dwarf has a mass similar to the Sun in a volume the size of Earth, making it extremely dense. White dwarfs are thought to be the final evolutionary state of stars whose mass is not high enough to become a neutron star or black hole.
