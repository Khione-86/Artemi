Venus

Venus is frequently referred to as Earth's "twin" or "sister" planet because it is an orbital neighbour and shares the most mass and size with Earth of all the planets in the Solar System. Venus has a far denser and thicker atmosphere than Earth and every other rocky planet in the Solar System, despite the fact that both are rocky. Its atmosphere is primarily made up of carbon dioxide (CO2), with no liquid water and a worldwide cloud blanket of sulphuric acid. The atmosphere's lowest layer of carbon dioxide becomes a supercritical fluid at mean surface level, when it achieves a temperature of 737 K (464 °C; 867 °F) and a pressure 92 times higher than Earth's at sea level.
